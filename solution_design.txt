1. PROBLEM IDENTIFICATION & OBJECTIVE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
On-street illegal parking and spillover parking near commercial hubs, transit
points, and intersections lead to severe reductions in carriageway capacity
and block major intersections. Today, traffic enforcement is reactive and
patrol-based. This solution aims to transition enforcement to a proactive,
data-driven model by:
  - Detecting illegal parking hotspots using spatial density clustering.
  - Quantifying the traffic congestion impact of each hotspot via a composite
    metric: the Congestion Impact Score (CIS).
  - Highlighting chronic areas and repeat offenders.
  - Providing an interactive, high-fidelity visual dashboard to prioritize
    and target enforcement resources.


2. DATA PIPELINE & PREPROCESSING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The raw traffic violation dataset contains spatial, temporal, vehicle, and
violation attributes. The preprocessing pipeline consists of:

  A. Data Cleaning & Parsing:
     - Map string representations of nulls (e.g., "NULL", "null", "") to np.nan.
     - Convert timestamps to timezone-aware datetime objects in Indian Standard
       Time (IST = UTC+5:30) to preserve local time contextual awareness.
     - Parse JSON-encoded violation lists (e.g. '["NO PARKING", "WRONG PARKING"]')
       into python lists of individual violations.

  B. Parking-Specific Filtering:
     - Identify parking-related records. Filter by checking if any item in the
       violation type list matches keywords:
       * "PARKING"
       * "NO PARKING"
       * "WRONG PARKING"
       * "PARKING IN A MAIN ROAD"
       * "PARKING NEAR ROAD CROSSING"
       * "PARKING ON FOOTPATH"
       * "PARKING IN NO PARKING ZONE"
     - Drop records missing latitude or longitude coordinates.

  C. Geofencing/Clamping:
     - Geofence data to the Bengaluru bounding box to filter out telemetry errors:
       * Latitude: [12.80, 13.35]
       * Longitude: [77.40, 77.80]


3. SPATIAL HOTSPOT DETECTION (DBSCAN CLUSTERING)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Due to the spatial nature of parking violations, grid-based clustering is
inadequate because it imposes artificial boundaries. Instead, we use DBSCAN
(Density-Based Spatial Clustering of Applications with Noise):

  - Distance Metric: Haversine distance in radians. This ensures accurate distance
    computations on the earth's curved surface.
  - Epsilon (eps): 150 meters. This corresponds to the typical walkability buffer
    and represents the physical span of a parking-induced congestion zone.
    * Calculation: eps_rad = (150 / 1000) / 6371.0 (Earth's radius in km)
  - Minimum Samples (MinPts): 8 violations. This acts as a threshold to filter out
    transient, isolated parking events and isolate statistically significant hotspots.
  - Output: Each violation is tagged with a cluster ID or marked as noise (-1).
    Noise points are discarded from hotspot analysis but kept for global stats.


4. CONGESTION IMPACT SCORE (CIS) FORMULATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
For each cluster C, we compute a composite Congestion Impact Score (CIS) to
quantify its severity. The CIS is composed of five sub-scores, each representing
a distinct operational dimension of parking-induced congestion:

  A. Spatial Density (S_density) - Weight: 30%
     - Measures how packed the violations are within the cluster.
     - Let N be the number of violations in the cluster. Let R_km be the maximum
       distance from the cluster centroid to any point in the cluster, floored at
       50 meters (0.05 km) to avoid division-by-zero and density spikes.
     - Area A = pi * (R_km)^2. Density D = N / A.
     - Because density is highly skewed (ranges from 10 to 10,000+), we take the
       logarithm to compress the range: S_density = log1p(D). We then apply
       MinMax scaling across all clusters to normalize to [0, 1].

  B. Junction Proximity (S_junction) - Weight: 25%
     - Parking near junctions severely blocks turning radiuses and cascades
       congestion across multiple road arms.
     - S_junction is the fraction of violations in the cluster that have a valid,
       non-trivial junction name associated with them (i.e. not "No Junction" or NaN).
     - Range: [0, 1].

  C. Vehicle Congestion Weight (S_vehicle) - Weight: 15%
     - Larger vehicles occupy more carriageway space. We map vehicle types to
       congestion weights:
       * BUS, TANKER, TRUCK, LORRY = 3.0
       * TIPPER = 2.5
       * MAXI-CAB = 2.0
       * VAN = 1.8
       * LMV, LMV-NT = 1.5
       * PASSENGER AUTO, CAR, JEEP, TAXI = 1.2
       * MOTOR CYCLE, SCOOTER, MOPED = 0.6
       * BICYCLE = 0.3
     - The cluster vehicle congestion weight is the mean weight of all vehicles
       in the cluster. We normalize this mean weight linearly between the minimum
       weight (0.3) and maximum weight (3.0) to get S_vehicle in [0, 1].

  D. Temporal Persistence (S_persistence) - Weight: 20%
     - Measures how chronic a hotspot is.
     - Let U_days be the number of unique days on which violations were recorded.
       Let T_span be the total date range of the cluster (latest date - earliest date + 1 day).
     - S_persistence = Min(U_days / T_span, 1.0).
     - Range: [0, 1].

  E. Peak Hour Overlap (S_peak) - Weight: 10%
     - Parking during peak hours causes exponentially more delay than off-peak.
     - Peak hours: Morning peak (07:00 - 10:00) and Evening peak (16:00 - 20:00).
     - S_peak is the fraction of violations in the cluster that occurred during
       these peak periods.
     - Range: [0, 1].

  F. Composite CIS Calculation:
     - Raw CIS = 0.30 * S_density_norm + 0.25 * S_junction + 0.15 * S_vehicle + 0.20 * S_persistence + 0.10 * S_peak
     - Final CIS = (Raw CIS / Max Raw CIS across all clusters) * 100
     - Sorted descending to yield a ranking from 1 (Most critical) to M (Least critical).

  G. Severity Tiering:
     - CRITICAL: CIS >= 70
     - HIGH: 45 <= CIS < 70
     - MEDIUM: 25 <= CIS < 45
     - LOW: CIS < 25


5. TRAFFIC QUANTIFICATION MODELS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
To move from a relative risk score (CIS) to a physical, real-world quantification
of traffic disruption, the pipeline implements four traffic engineering models
to quantify blockage severity:

  A. Passenger Car Unit (PCU) Blockage Load:
     - Standardizes the physical road space occupied by parked vehicles into Passenger
       Car Units (PCU) equivalent to standard cars (e.g., Bus/Truck = 3.0,
       Car/Jeep/Taxi = 1.2, Auto = 1.2, Two-Wheeler = 0.6).
     - Total Blockage Load (PCU) = Sum of (PCU Weight of all violations in cluster).

  B. Lane Capacity Reduction (LCR%):
     - Estimates the percentage reduction in traffic throughput capacity. A parking
       obstruction reduces effective carriageway width. If near a junction, this blockage
       impact is magnified due to turning movements blockage.
     - LCR = Min(15.0 + 35.0 * (Avg_PCU / 3.0) + 30.0 * Near_Junction, 85.0).
     - Range: [15%, 85%].

  C. Estimated Delay Multiplier (TTDF / BPR Index):
     - Models the exponential growth in vehicle travel times passing through the
       hotspot using the Bureau of Public Roads (BPR) delay formula:
       * Delay Multiplier = 1.0 + 0.15 * (1.0 / (1.0 - LCR / 100.0))^2
     - For example, a 50% capacity reduction (LCR=50%) results in a 1.6x increase in
       travel time, while an 80% reduction (LCR=80%) results in a 4.75x increase in
       travel time (creating massive queuing and spillover).

  D. Daily PCU Blockage Load (PCU-Hours / Day):
     - Estimates the daily aggregate traffic blockage load in PCU-hours:
       * Daily Load = (Total PCU Blockage / Active Days) * (0.5 + 1.5 * Peak Hour Fraction)
     - This scales the physical blockage by its average daily persistence and its
       overlap with peak demand periods.


6. REPEAT OFFENDER TRACKING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
To target enforcement against chronic individuals, we group violations by
vehicle number and cluster.
  - A vehicle is flagged as a Repeat Offender if it has 3 or more violations
     within the boundary of the same spatial hotspot.
  - The list is ordered by violation count descending to highlight chronic offenders.


7. ANALYTICS & WEB DASHBOARD VISUALIZATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
To present these findings to decision makers and patrol commanders, we build a
rich, interactive single-page application (SPA) with a dark theme and modern,
premium aesthetic:

  A. Interactive Map (Leaflet.js):
     - Displays all hotspots as circles styled by severity tier:
       * CRITICAL: Glowing Red (#ef4444)
       * HIGH: Orange (#f97316)
       * MEDIUM: Yellow (#eab308)
       * LOW: Blue/Cyan (#06b6d4)
     - Popup shows name, police station, CIS, number of violations, and peak hours.
     - Toggles between hotspot circle pins and a raw density Heatmap overlay
       (using leaflet-heat) to visualize macro vs. micro patterns.

  B. Filtering & Search Controls:
     - Filter by Police Station (dropdown with counts).
     - Filter by Severity Tier.
     - Search by location name/junction.

  C. Dashboard Panels:
     - KPI Summary Cards: Total Violations, Hotspots, Critical Zones, Average CIS,
       and Peak Hour.
     - Hotspot Leaderboard: Prioritized list of hotspots sorted by CIS. Clicking
       a card pans and zooms the map to that hotspot's centroid.
     - Detailed Hotspot Analysis:
       * Shows the breakdown of sub-scores (Density, Junction, Vehicle Weight,
         Persistence, Peak) using animated progress bars.
       * Shows the direct Traffic Quantification KPIs (Total PCU Blockage, Daily
         Blockage Load, Capacity Loss, and Travel Time Delay Index).
     - Global Temporal Charts (Chart.js):
       * Hourly distribution (diurnal patterns).
       * Day of week distribution (weekly patterns).
       * Monthly trends (long-term patterns).
     - Repeat Offender Leaderboard: High-frequency offenders inside hotspots.
